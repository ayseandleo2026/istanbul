What to Visit in Istanbul

A few easy routes, plus some food and ferry tips.

If you stay at Fuat Pasa

There is a ferry stop right in front of the hotel, with departures throughout the day. It’s a beautiful way to reach the Bosphorus line and Eminönü.

Ferry schedule: Şehir Hatları timetable (Rumelikavağı → Eminönü)

Tip: Istanbul traffic is real. When it fits your plan, the ferry is often simpler than driving.

Route 1

Old Town in one walk

  • Hagia Sophia
  • Blue Mosque (Sultan Ahmed Mosque)
  • Topkapi Palace
  • All in the same area, easy to visit together.
  • Optional add on: Basilica Cistern (quick and impressive).

Route 2

Markets and views

  • İstiklal Caddesi
  • Galata Tower and the small arty streets around it
  • Grand Bazaar and Spice Bazaar
  • Karaköy
  • Baklava stop: Karaköy Güllüoğlu. Open link

Bosphorus

If you stay elsewhere and won’t use the local ferry for commuting, a Bosphorus ferry tour is highly recommended. Sunset hours are especially nice.

  • Ortaköy for a quick stop and waterfront views
  • Dolmabahçe Palace if you want one more big historical visit

Breakfast

Turkish breakfast is part of daily life. Don’t leave without having one. Pick a place near you, or go for the unlimited style at Bazlama Kahvaltı (many locations).

Food and a real night out

Turkish cuisine is much more than kebab. For a classic experience, try Rakı with meze, then kebab.

If you have extra time

  • Kadıköy for markets, food, and a different side of the city
  • Balat for colorful streets and slow wandering
  • Princes’ Islands for a full day escape (when weather is good)

One classic experience

A hammam session is a very Istanbul thing to do. If you want to book one, choose a well reviewed historic hammam and reserve ahead.
